Historical timeline

The memory that refused to die.

Follow the line. See what survived.

681

The State

Khan Asparuh establishes the Bulgarian state.

By the Danube, a federation of Bulgars and Slavs forms the first Bulgarian state — recognized by the Byzantine Empire after a decisive military victory. The name Bulgaria enters history. A name that will survive everything that follows.

862

The Letters

A new alphabet is born for the Slavic peoples.

Two brothers from Thessaloniki create the Glagolitic script — the foundation of the later Cyrillic alphabet. For the first time, the Slavic world has letters of its own. Bulgaria will adopt and preserve this legacy for centuries.

865

The Baptism

Khan Boris I accepts Christianity for Bulgaria.

In a calculated political act of profound cultural consequence, Khan Boris I converts Bulgaria to Christianity. A new identity forms around faith and literacy. Pliska and Preslav become centers of Slavic Christian civilization.

893

The Language

Old Bulgarian becomes the official language of state and church.

Under Tsar Simeon I, the Preslav Literary School flourishes. Old Bulgarian replaces Greek in all official contexts. Bulgaria becomes a literary and spiritual powerhouse at the center of medieval Europe.

1018

The Fall

Bulgaria falls — but the name endures.

Basil II conquers Bulgaria after decades of war. The Bulgarian state ceases to exist for 167 years. Yet the name, the language, and the memory survive — carried in monasteries, in villages, in oral tradition, in the stones themselves.

1185

The Return

The Second Bulgarian Empire rises from Tarnovo.

Brothers Asen and Peter lead an uprising from the Tarnovo hills. Bulgaria is reborn — the only medieval Slavic state to reconstitute itself after complete conquest. The name survived. The memory returned. The journey continues.
