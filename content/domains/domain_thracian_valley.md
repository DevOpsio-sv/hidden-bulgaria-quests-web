## Concept

The Thracian Valley is the oldest inhabited landscape in Bulgaria — possibly in all of Europe. This is where Thracian kings were buried under earthen mounds filled with gold. Where the rose was cultivated into the most valuable perfume ingredient in the world. Where Plovdiv — one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in Europe — contains Roman, Greek, Bulgarian, Ottoman, and modern layers compressed into a single living city. The Domain of Kings asks: what was here before 681? And why does it still matter?

---

## Historical Epoch

**Before the Name (Преди Името)** — with deep Thracian prehistory (5th century BC and earlier) as the foundation.

The Thracian civilisation predates the Bulgarian state by over a thousand years. Yet Bulgarian national identity has incorporated Thracian heritage as part of its own story — the Thracian kings of the Valley are now considered part of Bulgaria's pre-history, even though they never knew the name.

## The Three Routes

### Route 1 — `route_thracian_valley_tombs`
**Working title:** The Gold Under the Mound  
**Narrative:** Over 1500 Thracian burial mounds have been found in the Kazanlak area alone. The Kazanlak Tomb (UNESCO World Heritage) contains the finest preserved Thracian frescoes in the world. The Valley of the Thracian Kings is not a metaphor — it is a real concentration of royal burial sites, some still unexcavated.  
**Key checkpoint locations (draft):**
- Kazanlak Tomb (UNESCO — original closed, replica accessible)
- Golyama Kosmatka Tomb (discovery of the Thracian king Seuthes III mask)
- Shipka Pass memorial (where Bulgarian freedom was defended in 1877 — connecting Thracian prehistory to modern Bulgarian identity)
- Regional History Museum Kazanlak (Thracian gold artifacts)
- Seuthopolis (submerged Thracian city site — visible from boat in Koprinka Reservoir)

**Route Relic (draft):** `relic_golden_mask_of_the_valley`

---

### Route 2 — `route_thracian_valley_roses`
**Working title:** The Memory That Smells  
**Narrative:** The Rose Valley between Kazanlak and Karlovo produces 70-85% of the world's rose oil. The Damask rose was introduced to Bulgaria via Ottoman trade routes, but the tradition of cultivation and distillation became so uniquely Bulgarian that "Bulgarian rose oil" is a geographic indication. The rose is the most economically significant sensory memory in Bulgaria.  
**Key checkpoint locations (draft):**
- Rose festival area in Kazanlak (early June — harvest season)
- Damascena Ethnographic Complex (working rose distillery)
- Karlovo — birthplace of Vasil Levski (connecting rose country to Bulgarian national revival)
- Sopot (the rose oil museum)
- Valley viewpoint (the rose fields themselves at harvest season)

**Route Relic (draft):** `relic_rose_oil_vial`

---

### Route 3 — `route_thracian_valley_plovdiv`
**Working title:** The City That Never Stopped  
**Narrative:** Plovdiv (ancient Philippopolis, before that Eumolpias) is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in Europe — over 8000 years of continuous habitation. Romans, Greeks, Byzantines, Bulgarians, Ottomans, and a modern European Capital of Culture 2019 — Plovdiv is the most direct illustration of how culture layers accumulate without destroying what came before.  
**Key checkpoint locations (draft):**
- Plovdiv Ancient Theatre (Roman, 2nd century AD — still in active use for concerts)
- Plovdiv Old Town (Kapana and Hisar districts — Bulgarian National Revival architecture)
- Philippopolis ruins (Roman forum visible beneath the modern city)
- Nebet Tepe (Thracian hill — the oldest part of the city, inhabited since 6000 BC)
- Plovdiv Regional Archaeological Museum (the Panagyurishte Gold Treasure — finest Thracian gold found in Bulgaria)

**Route Relic (draft):** `relic_thracian_rhyton`

## Domain Seal

**Seal ID:** `seal_thracian_valley`  
**Working concept:** The seal is based on the deer-head rhyton from the Panagyurishte Gold Treasure — one of the most iconic Thracian artifacts and a symbol of the Valley's golden memory. The circular seal echoes the shape of the Kazanlak Tomb's burial chamber ceiling, itself a mandala of ancient Bulgarian-Thracian continuity.

---

## Narrative Arc Across the Domain

**Act 1 (Tombs route):** The kings who ruled before the name. Their gold is still here.  
**Act 2 (Roses route):** The plant that arrived from the east and became the most Bulgarian thing in Bulgaria.  
**Act 3 (Plovdiv route):** The city that survived every civilization by becoming part of each one.

The domain's answer to the core question: *The name survived because the land was already ancient when it arrived. Bulgaria inherited a landscape with thousands of years of memory already buried in it. The Thracian kings, the rose fields, the layered city — all of this was absorbed into Bulgarian identity. The name didn't replace what was here. It continued it.*

## Content Production Notes

- Panagyurishte Gold Treasure is internationally famous — one of the most recognizable Thracian artifacts globally
- Plovdiv's 2019 European Capital of Culture status created international media coverage that can be referenced
- The Rose Valley story has strong sensory and economic hooks — accessible to all audiences, not just history enthusiasts
- Shipka Pass connects the Thracian epoch directly to Bulgarian national history — bridging "Before the Name" and "After the Name" in a single location
- Seuthopolis (the submerged Thracian city) has a pending plan to surround it with a circular dam to make it visible again — if this project proceeds, it would be an extraordinary AR site
