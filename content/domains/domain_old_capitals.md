---

## Concept

This is the domain that answers the core question most directly. It follows the three capitals that carried Bulgaria's name through conquest, fire, and rebirth: Pliska (the founding capital), Preslav (the golden age), and Tarnovo (the fortress of resurrection). Together, the three cities tell the story of how a state can fall and rise again — and still remember its own name.

---

## Historical Epoch

**After the Name (След Името)** — 681 AD to present.

The domain opens at the founding of the Bulgarian state (681 AD) and traces the institutional survival of the name through the First Bulgarian Empire, the Byzantine conquest of 1018, and the restoration of 1185.

## The Three Routes

### Route 1 — `route_old_capitals_pliska`
**Working title:** The First Name  
**Narrative:** How Khan Asparuh established Bulgaria at the Danube and made the name official. Five checkpoints across the Pliska archaeological complex and surroundings.  
**Key checkpoint locations (draft):**
- Pliska archaeological site (outer city)
- The Great Basilica of Pliska
- Madara Rider (UNESCO World Heritage Site)
- Shumen Plateau fortress
- Memorial of the Founders of the Bulgarian State

**Route Relic (draft):** `relic_first_seal_of_the_name`

---

### Route 2 — `route_old_capitals_preslav`
**Working title:** The Golden Letters  
**Narrative:** How Tsar Simeon I made Old Bulgarian the language of state and created the Preslav Literary School — turning a political name into a cultural civilization.  
**Key checkpoint locations (draft):**
- Preslav archaeological site (capital walls)
- The Round Golden Church of Preslav
- Preslav inscription wall (Cyrillic origins)
- Preslav Museum (the Golden Treasury)
- Patleyna monastery complex

**Route Relic (draft):** `relic_golden_letter_fragment`

---

### Route 3 — `route_old_capitals_tarnovo`
**Working title:** The Return from Darkness  
**Narrative:** How the Asen brothers led the uprising from the Tarnovo hills in 1185, restoring the Bulgarian state after 167 years of Byzantine rule. The only medieval Slavic state to reconstitute itself after complete conquest.  
**Key checkpoint locations (draft):**
- Tsarevets fortress (main gate)
- Patriarchate Church of the Ascension
- Trapezitsa fortress hill
- Holy Forty Martyrs Church (Asen Brothers inscription)
- Arbanasi (Ottoman-era Bulgarian cultural preservation)

**Route Relic (draft):** `relic_banner_of_resurrection`

## Domain Seal

**Seal ID:** `seal_old_capitals`  
**Working concept:** The seal combines the emblems of all three capitals — the Madara Rider's spear, Preslav's circular golden church plan, and the Tsarevets fortress silhouette — into a single composite symbol representing unbroken continuity.

---

## Narrative Arc Across the Domain

The domain follows a three-act survival structure:

**Act 1 (Pliska route):** The name is born. A state is declared. The Danube holds.  
**Act 2 (Preslav route):** The name creates. Literature, faith, and language forge an identity that outlasts the state.  
**Act 3 (Tarnovo route):** The name returns. After conquest and silence, it rises again — unchanged.

The domain's answer to the core question: *The name survived because it became more than a political claim. It became a cultural identity embedded in language, faith, and memory — impossible to conquer because it lived in people, not only in capitals.*
