How it works

Become a Keeper,
one place at a time.

Every journey in Unlocking Bulgaria follows a clear progression — from a single checkpoint to the full unlock of Bulgaria's hidden memory.

  1. Step 1

    Visit a Checkpoint

    Travel to a real place. Activate the checkpoint by arriving at the location. Unlock a story, a clue, or a hidden layer.

  2. Step 2 — 5 Checkpoints

    Complete a Route → Collect a Relic

    Five connected checkpoints form a Route. Complete the route to receive a permanent digital relic — an artifact of Bulgaria's history.

  3. Step 3 — 3 Routes

    Unlock a Domain → Earn a Seal

    Three routes unlock a regional Domain. Earn its Seal — a symbol of mastery over that region's hidden memory.

  4. Step 4 — 6 Domains

    National Unlock → Become a Keeper

    Complete all six domains. Unlock the full memory of Bulgaria. Receive the title of Keeper — Пазител.

Historical timeline

The memory that survived.

1345 years of Bulgarian history — compressed into the moments that defined the name, the faith, and the language.

681

Foundation of Bulgaria

Khan Asparuh establishes the Bulgarian state.

By the Danube, a federation of Bulgars and Slavs forms the first Bulgarian state recognized by the Byzantine Empire. The name Bulgaria enters history. A name that will survive everything that comes next.

862

Saints Cyril & Methodius

A new alphabet is born for the Slavic peoples.

Two brothers from Thessaloniki create the Glagolitic script — the foundation of the later Cyrillic alphabet. For the first time, the Slavic world has letters of its own. Bulgaria will adopt and preserve them.

865

Baptism of Bulgaria

Khan Boris I accepts Christianity.

In a calculated political act of profound consequence, Khan Boris I converts Bulgaria to Christianity. A new cultural identity forms. The Pliska and Preslav courts become centers of Slavic Christian civilization.

893

Preslav Council

Old Bulgarian becomes the official language of state and church.

Under Tsar Simeon I, the Preslav Literary School flourishes. Old Bulgarian replaces Greek in all official contexts. Bulgaria becomes a literary and spiritual powerhouse of medieval Europe.

1018

Byzantine Conquest

Bulgaria falls — but the name endures.

Basil II conquers Bulgaria and blinds 15,000 soldiers. The Bulgarian state ceases to exist — yet the name, the language, and the memory survive in monasteries, villages, and oral tradition. For 167 years.

1185

The Asen Brothers

The Second Bulgarian Empire rises from Tarnovo.

Brothers Asen and Peter lead an uprising from the Tarnovo hills. Bulgaria is reborn — the only medieval Slavic state to re-establish itself after complete conquest. The name survived. The memory returned.
